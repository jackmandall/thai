For the two of you

On the three 777 Qsuite legs, book the centre pair of suites (the middle two). They have a lowerable divider and convert into a shared double bed. On the 787-9 Gatwick leg the centre two sit together (both fully flat, both with doors) but without the double-bed conversion.

Caveat: airlines can swap aircraft up to departure, and these routes are not on Qatar's guaranteed all-Qsuite list, so check the seat map once ticketed and again nearer travel.

The hotel — The Slate, Nai Yang

A genuinely special, characterful 5-star: an all-suite Bill Bensley design themed on Phuket's tin-mining history, with several pools, eight restaurants and bars, a strong spa, and consistently high marks for the rooms, breakfast and service. Your Pearl Bed Suite is a generous 56m² (garden and pool view, not sea view). It is roughly five minutes from the airport, which suits your 06:50 arrival and post-midnight departure nicely.

The one real caveat is location. It sits up at Nai Yang in the far north, beside a quiet, unspoilt beach. Lovely for calm, but it is well away from Phuket's dining and nightlife: the trendy beach clubs and best restaurants (Surin and Bang Tao) are about 30 to 40 minutes south, and Patong and the Old Town further still. Nai Yang itself is sleepy, a few low-key beach bars and local food stalls.

My verdict

My steer The flights are excellent and I would happily approve those. On the hotel, I would not book The Slate for the two of you unless you specifically want a quiet, design-led, do-very-little week. Going on the kind of trips you enjoy, with good food and a bit of buzz in reach, its far-north isolation is a mismatch: you would be taxiing 30 to 40 minutes most evenings to eat and drink well.

Better suited: base in Bang Tao or Surin. Same west coast, about 25 minutes from the airport, but on Phuket's best stretch for couples, walkable to top beach clubs (Catch, Carpe Diem, the new RAVA) and a deep choice of restaurants, while still relaxed. Picks, roughly in order:

  • Twinpalms Surin — the like-for-like swap in style: chic, superb food and pool scene, and it owns Catch Beach Club just down the road. The natural couples' choice.
  • Rosewood Phuket (Emerald Bay) — a step up in luxury and seclusion, beautiful villas and beach, still near Surin dining. Likely dearer than the current package.
  • Anantara Layan — secluded, romantic and indulgent if you want quiet luxury but better dining than Nai Yang.

All three are swappable in the same BA Holidays basket via the hotel "Change" link; the price will move with the choice. If you do want pure calm and the airport convenience, The Slate is a fine pick and I would not talk you out of it, it is just not the one I would choose for your tastes.

Caveats to keep in mind

  • Out of Gatwick, back into Heathrow — different London airports, so plan parking and transport accordingly.
  • Overnight return: Phuket departs 00:50, so you leave the hotel late on the 23rd. You keep the room through the 23rd, so no day-room needed, but worth a relaxed evening plan.
  • Early arrival: you land Phuket 06:50 on the 15th, so request early check-in or expect to wait until around 2pm for the room.
  • Thailand entry: UK passports get 60 days visa-free, but you will need a Thailand Digital Arrival Card (online shortly before travel) and at least six months' passport validity. Check gov.uk nearer the time.
  • Vaping is illegal in Thailand — e-cigarettes and refills can be confiscated with fines, so leave them at home.
  • Cancellation bites: £700 deposit lost if you cancel, then 60% from seven weeks out, 100% inside 72 hours. Sale price flagged to end midnight 25 June.
  • Good news: none of the five Thai Buddhist alcohol-restriction days fall in your window, transfers are included, and the booking earns serious BA tier points.
